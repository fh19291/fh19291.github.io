   Situation
A regional retail company was consolidating three legacy inventory systems into a single platform across 12 stores and a central warehouse. The initiative touched Finance, Store Operations, and IT simultaneously — three workstreams with different priorities, no shared governance model, and a history of projects stalling because no one had clear decision rights. My task was to stand up the program's governance structure before any execution work began, so that scope, accountability, and reporting cadence were locked in from day one.

What I Built
1. Program Charter (Word)
A 2-page charter defining:

Program objective: consolidate 3 inventory systems into 1 platform, reducing reconciliation time from 4 hours/week per store to under 30 minutes
Scope boundaries: what's in (data migration, staff retraining, POS integration) and explicitly out (no changes to supplier contracts)
Success criteria: 100% store cutover within 6 months, <2% data discrepancy post-migration, zero unplanned downtime during business hours
Governance structure: Steering Committee (biweekly), Program Lead (weekly), Workstream Leads (daily standups)

2. RACI Matrix (Excel)
A single-tab matrix mapping 8 key decisions (budget approval, cutover go/no-go, vendor selection, training sign-off, etc.) against 6 roles (Sponsor, Program Lead, 3 Workstream Leads, IT Security). This resolved the recurring problem of decisions stalling because no one knew who had final say.
3. Master Plan + RAID Log (Smartsheet)
A rolled-up plan showing all three workstreams on one timeline, with a linked RAID log tracking:

Risks: e.g., "Store staff availability during peak season may delay training" (Owner: Ops Lead, Mitigation: schedule training in Q1 shoulder season)
Assumptions: e.g., "All stores have adequate network bandwidth for real-time sync"
Issues: escalated items with resolution owner and target date
Dependencies: e.g., "POS integration cannot begin until data migration is validated"

4. Execution Layer (JIRA)
Three linked epics — one per workstream (Data Migration, Store Enablement, IT Integration) — broken into stories with acceptance criteria, so the governance-level plan in Smartsheet had a direct line down to daily execution tickets.
5. Steering Committee Update (PowerPoint)
A one-page executive status format used biweekly: RAG status by workstream, top 3 risks, decisions needed from the committee, and a rolling timeline view. Designed so a sponsor could read it in under 90 seconds.
Tools Used
Microsoft Word Microsoft Excel Microsoft PowerPoint Smartsheet JIRA
Why It Matters for a Program Manager
Individual projects fail from bad execution; programs fail from bad governance — unclear ownership, no shared view across workstreams, and reporting that doesn't reach the right altitude for each audience. This case study isn't about knowing how to use Word or JIRA — it's about designing the structure that lets three independent teams move as one program, with escalation paths and decision rights defined before the first ticket is ever created. That's the shift from managing a project to managing a program.
